Taking a Stand: Shang Yang's Reform

Background
  1. During Warring States Period (475 BC - 221 BC), the feudal system had collapsed, and the status of the aristocrats declined while that of the ordinary people rose
  2. Duke Xiao of Qin started to recruit talented people in order to strengthen Qin
  3. Shang Yang went from Wei and persuaded Duke Xiao of Qin to conduct reform

LAND REFORM
  1. Shang Yang abandoned the well-field system​ and admitted land privatization
  2. He permitted ordinary people to settle and farm new lands and promoted free commerce of land
  3. The reform encouraged the motivation of private farmers to produce more and even offered a stable supply of resource for Qin during wars
Farming Reform
  1. Shang Yang assisted agriculture yet discouraged commerce
  2. He carried out united tax system
  3. The reform weakened the privilege of aristocrats and made those nobles be involved in the farming production
Family Reform
  1. In order to boost peasant economy, Shang Yang implemented the policy of nuclear family (one wife and one husband)
  2. The government could collect more revenue since the tax was based on each household
  3. The population in Qin increased
Measurement Reform
  1. Shang Yang unified measurement system in Qin
  2. The reform enabled Qin to be more united
  3. The government could collect revenue in various regions more easily
Military Reform
  1. Shang Yang enacted a military exploit system (people who had higher military exploit could be granted with higher title)
  2. The reform diminished the status of old hereditary aristocrats and encouraged the emergence of new landed gentries
  3. It also punished regional fight severely

Collateral Punishment
  1. Shang Yang organized a Hukou system of 伍(five households) and 什 (ten households) as basic administrative units
  2. Residents must supervise each other
  3. If a crime of a household in 伍 or 什 was not informed by others, all the residents in the units would be punished
The System of Prefecture and County
  1. Shang Yang arranged Qin as a state composed of prefectures and counties
  2. He abolished the feudal system, which the authority was divided by vassals
  3. The reform contributed to centralization since the county magistrates were appointed by the central government

Conclusion
  1. Shang Yang’s reform promoted the development in Qin
  2. The reform laid the foundation for the powerful state Qin to conquer others and founded Qin Dynasty
